Memebear

Like some other creators of analogue horror videos, Memebear used the Five Nights at Freddy's series of horror games for the basis of the series. Possibly based upon the series' unintended attraction to younger people, Memebear's videos take on an increased horror level, using elements of body horror and exploiting phobias for some of the imagery, such as scopophobia (the fear of being stared at). Memebear also chose to stick with the jumpscare style of horror also, and chose to not have massive elements of story to the videos, so that they could be percieved as being a part of the Five Nights at Freddy's story.
Many of the videos on Memebear's channel use .mp4 as a suffix to the title, further referencing to the analogue genre and increasing the believability to the video. One of the videos, Office_Footage.mp4, shows a dark, open office with a large hallway in the centre, with a static filter on the video. After the camera pans to the sides, a bright figure appears off into the distance of the hallway, looking like a distorted image of a child's face. Using this to entice viewers to look closer, some sort of singing or lullaby can be heard as the image clears up slightly. The words 'I'm coming for you' are suddenly spoken, before the face disappears and the music is replaced with heavy footsteps, and an animatronic bear sprints towards the camera.
Another one of the videos, nightmare.mp4, which is labelled as non-canon to Memebear's view of the story. This video instead takes perspective in a bedroom, with the perspective likely being that of a small child. The child looks around the room a few times, until the perspective turns around to the bed, with a distorted bear plush sitting infront of the camera. The camera quickly pans to check the room again, before breathing is heard, and the plush transforms into a yellow bear head. The torch light dies out, as the child breathes rapidly and proceeds to be attacked by the bear. The screen pauses before cutting to an 80's era recording of the fixed bear at a presentation, before its view meets the camera and the screen cuts to later, where an accident has occured. Police sirens are heard as the bear stays motionless, with a bloodied lower jaw and something in its mouth.
